# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two visitor groups arriving at the same page:

- **Homeowners in Lahore** evaluating who to trust with a major, expensive home upgrade. They want lower bills and reliable power during outages, but the bigger question is who they can trust to install a system that won't be a problem in two years. Reading on phones, often in the evening, comparing two or three installers in the same session.
- **Small and medium businesses** (shops, factories, offices) priced out by grid tariffs and load-shedding. They care about payback period, system uptime, and whether the team can deliver at commercial scale. Decision often involves more than one person on the buy side.

Both arrive cold from word-of-mouth, search, or a referral. The job to be done is the same: decide whether Voltara is worth a real conversation.

## Product Purpose

A single-page brand site that earns enough trust, fast enough, to get a qualified visitor into a WhatsApp conversation. Success is measured by quality leads landing in WhatsApp / the contact form, not pageviews. The page is the top of the funnel; the actual sale happens 1:1 with a human after the click.

It must speak to both residential and commercial buyers without retreating to category-neutral "energy solutions" language that resonates with neither.

## Brand Personality

Premium, trustworthy, established. Voltara should read like a serious, long-running energy firm — confident, calm, considered — not a Silicon Valley startup and not a local template-site installer. Editorial pacing, generous whitespace, deliberate motion. The serif typography (Libre Baskerville) and cream/navy/gold palette are load-bearing identity choices.

Voice is direct and unembellished. Specific over aspirational ("Lahore's trusted solar team" beats "transforming Pakistan's energy future"). No hard-sell, no urgency tricks, no marketing buzzwords.

## Anti-references

- **Tesla / SunRun / SaaS-tech aesthetic** is the explicit anti-reference. No dark hero with neon gradients. No gradient text. No glassy/translucent card grids as scaffolding. No generic "clean energy product page" composition.
- Avoid the local-installer template-site look as a secondary risk: stock panels-on-rooftop photography, three-color WordPress theme, builder-grade testimonial sliders.
- No eco-cliché either: leaf iconography, soft green gradients, sunrise stock, "sustainable future" platitudes.
- No hard-sell sales-page energy: countdown timers, ALL-CAPS urgency, yellow highlight slabs, multiple competing CTAs above the fold.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust earns the click.** Premium typography, considered motion, real specifics — every element should reinforce that this team is established and serious. If a component reads as decorative, it's working against the goal.
2. **One conversation, one door.** The page exists to start a WhatsApp conversation. The CTA hierarchy on every section must reflect that; secondary actions support the primary, they don't compete with it.
3. **Specific, not aspirational.** Concrete claims (Lahore, names, system sizes, real installs) beat abstract promises. No marketing buzzwords; no aphoristic copy cadence.
4. **Speak to both buyers without homogenizing.** Residential and commercial visitors land on the same page. The copy and proof must address both without collapsing into generic "energy solutions" language that resonates with neither.
5. **Motion is gravitas, not flash.** Animations should pace the reveal of meaning, not announce themselves. The existing hero intro sets the bar — slow, eased, deliberate. Don't stagger-reveal every section.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA across the site. Specifically:

- Body text ≥4.5:1 against its background; large/heading text ≥3:1. Verify on cream and over imagery.
- Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states (not the default browser ring on a cream background — design one).
- Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative. The hero intro already respects this; hold the line for new motion.
- WhatsApp and contact CTAs must be reachable and operable without a pointer.
- Mixed-language audience (English-first, Urdu-comfortable readers): keep copy plain and avoid idioms that don't translate.
